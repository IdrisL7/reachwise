# Hook Test Scenarios — Phase 1

Use these after the provider window resets to test whether Phase 1 improvements are reducing generic AI phrasing and improving buyer tension.

## Test Instructions

For each scenario:

1. Generate hooks for the specified company and role.
2. Save the top 3 hooks.
3. Score them with [hook-quality-rubric.md](/Users/driss/Downloads/Example/docs/hook-quality-rubric.md).
4. Note any repeated generic phrases or weak promises.

## Scenario 1

- Company: Notion
- Role: VP Sales
- Signal type: IPO / valuation / investor event
- What to watch:
  - avoid `board scrutiny` filler
  - avoid article-summary framing
  - push toward forecast confidence, deal risk, inspection cadence

## Scenario 2

- Company: HubSpot
- Role: RevOps
- Signal type: expansion / multi-region growth
- What to watch:
  - routing, data quality, reporting reliability
  - avoid generic “scaling creates complexity” lines

## Scenario 3

- Company: Gong
- Role: SDR Manager
- Signal type: hiring / team growth
- What to watch:
  - ramp drag, coaching leverage, execution consistency
  - avoid generic “hiring creates pressure” wording

## Scenario 4

- Company: Motive
- Role: Marketing
- Signal type: partnership / customer proof / product expansion
- What to watch:
  - handoff quality, lead leakage, campaign efficiency
  - avoid forcing sales-heavy language into marketing hooks

## Scenario 5

- Company: Datadog
- Role: Founder/CEO
- Signal type: recognition / growth milestone
- What to watch:
  - growth complexity, operating visibility, efficiency
  - avoid empty praise like “Congrats on…”

## Scenario 6

- Role: VP Sales

## Scenario 7

## Scenario 8

## Scenario 9

## Scenario 10
